# Ticket 001: Swing strategy backtest

We want a basic swing trading strategy that buys when the trend turns up
and sells when it turns down, using moving averages. It should size
positions based on how much we're willing to risk, and get out if the
trade goes against us too much. At the end we want to know how many
trades it made, how often it won, and what the overall profit and loss
looked like.

---

## Tasks

- Implement a moving-average crossover signal generator (fast MA crossing above slow MA = uptrend/buy signal; fast MA crossing below slow MA = downtrend/sell signal).
- Implement position sizing based on a configurable risk-per-trade percentage of account equity and the distance to the stop-loss.
- Implement a stop-loss mechanism that exits a position when the trade moves against it beyond a defined risk threshold.
- Implement a backtest engine that runs the strategy over historical daily price bars and produces fills without look-ahead bias.
- Implement a results/reporting summary at the end of a backtest run: total trade count, win rate, and overall profit and loss.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. The strategy uses two simple moving averages (SMA), a fast length of 10 bars and a slow length of 20 bars, computed on daily closing price.
2. No signal may be generated until at least 20 bars (the slow SMA's warmup period) of price history are available.
3. A buy signal is generated on the bar where the fast SMA crosses from at-or-below to strictly above the slow SMA.
4. A sell (exit) signal is generated on the bar where the fast SMA crosses from at-or-above to strictly below the slow SMA.
5. A signal computed from a given bar's close must be filled at the next bar's open, never at the close of the same bar that generated it (no look-ahead bias).
6. Only one open position may exist at a time per instrument; a buy signal occurring while a position is already open is ignored.
7. Position size for a new trade is calculated as: `(account equity × risk percentage) / (entry price − stop-loss price)`, rounded down to a whole share/unit.
8. The default risk percentage per trade is 1% of current account equity.
9. The stop-loss price for a new long position is set at 2× the 14-period Average True Range (ATR) below the entry price, calculated at entry time.
10. If a bar's low reaches or breaches the position's stop-loss price, the position is closed at the stop-loss price on that same bar (stop-loss fills do not wait for the next bar's open).
11. A trade's profit and loss (P&L) is calculated in both absolute currency terms and as an R-multiple (P&L ÷ initial risk amount).
12. At the end of a backtest run, the engine reports: total number of closed trades, win rate (percentage of trades with P&L > 0), and total/net P&L across all trades.
13. A trade with P&L exactly equal to 0 is not counted as a win when calculating win rate.
14. All position sizing and P&L calculations are implemented as pure functions with no side effects, taking price/account state as explicit inputs.

## Decisions made to resolve ambiguity

- **Moving average type and lengths**: The request only says "moving averages" — assumed simple moving averages (SMA) with a 10/20-day fast/slow crossover pair, a common default for swing strategies. This should be confirmed or adjusted by the reviewer.
- **Fill price convention**: Assumed signals fill at the next bar's open (standard no-look-ahead convention), since the request doesn't specify.
- **Risk-based position sizing formula**: The request says "size positions based on how much we're willing to risk" but doesn't define the risk percentage or stop distance. Assumed 1% of equity risked per trade, with stop distance derived from ATR.
- **Stop-loss definition**: "Gets out if the trade goes against us too much" was interpreted as a volatility-based stop (2× 14-period ATR below entry) rather than a fixed percentage or dollar stop, since ATR-based stops are standard for swing trading and adapt to instrument volatility. This is a significant assumption and should be explicitly confirmed.
- **Long-only scope**: The request mentions buying on uptrend and selling on downtrend, which was interpreted as a long-only strategy (buy and later exit), not a short-selling strategy. Flagged for confirmation since "sells when it turns down" is ambiguous between "exit long" and "enter short."
- **Single position constraint**: Assumed the strategy trades one instrument with at most one open position at a time, since the request gives no detail on portfolio-level behavior or multiple concurrent positions.
