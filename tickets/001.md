# Ticket 001: Swing strategy backtest

We want a basic swing trading strategy that buys when the trend turns up
and sells when it turns down, using moving averages. It should size
positions based on how much we're willing to risk, and get out if the
trade goes against us too much. At the end we want to know how many
trades it made, how often it won, and what the overall profit and loss
looked like.

---

## Tasks

- Implement a moving-average crossover signal generator (fast MA crosses above slow MA → long entry signal; fast MA crosses below slow MA → exit signal).
- Implement a position-sizing function that computes share quantity from account equity, a fixed risk-per-trade percentage, and the stop-loss distance.
- Implement a stop-loss mechanism that exits an open position if price moves against it beyond a defined threshold.
- Implement a backtest loop that walks historical daily bars, applies the signal/sizing/stop logic bar-by-bar without look-ahead bias, and records trades.
- Implement a results summary: total trade count, win rate, and total P&L (plus per-trade P&L needed to derive these).

## Acceptance Criteria

1. The strategy uses two simple moving averages (SMA), a fast length of 20 and a slow length of 50, computed on daily close prices.
2. No signal can be generated until both SMAs have full warmup data (at least 50 closes available); bars before that must produce no signal.
3. A long entry signal fires on the first bar where the fast SMA crosses from at-or-below to above the slow SMA (a "golden cross").
4. An exit signal fires on the first bar where the fast SMA crosses from at-or-above to below the slow SMA (a "death cross"), closing any open long position.
5. A signal computed from bar N's close must be filled at bar N+1's open, never at bar N's close (no look-ahead bias).
6. Only one position may be open at a time; entry signals while a position is already open are ignored.
7. Position size is calculated as: `shares = (accountEquity * riskPercentPerTrade) / (entryPrice - stopLossPrice)`, rounded down to a whole share.
8. Risk per trade is fixed at 1% of current account equity.
9. The stop-loss price for a new position is set at entry using a fixed 5% below the entry fill price.
10. Exit checks run in a fixed order each bar: first, if a death-cross signal was generated on the previous bar's close, the position closes at this bar's open (per the fill-timing rule in #5); then, only if the position is still open afterward, if this bar's low reaches or breaches the stop-loss price, the position closes at the stop-loss price on this same bar. The exit reason recorded is whichever check actually triggered the close.
11. Each closed trade records: entry date/price, exit date/price, exit reason (signal vs. stop-loss), shares, and realized P&L.
12. At the end of a backtest run, the engine reports: total number of closed trades, win rate (% of trades with positive P&L), and total P&L summed across all trades.
13. A trade with zero P&L counts as a loss (not a win) for win-rate purposes.
14. All money-math functions (sizing, stop calculation, P&L, win-rate aggregation) are pure functions with no I/O, taking inputs and returning outputs only.

## Decisions made to resolve ambiguity

- **Moving average type and lengths**: The request said "moving averages" with no specifics. Assumed simple moving averages (SMA) with a 20/50 fast/slow crossover — a common, simple default. A stakeholder may want EMA or different lengths.
- **Fill price convention**: The request didn't say when a signal becomes a fill. Assumed next-bar-open fills to satisfy the project's no-look-ahead-bias rule (CLAUDE.md).
- **Position sizing / risk %**: The request said "size positions based on how much we're willing to risk" but gave no number. Assumed a fixed 1% of equity risked per trade — a common default risk unit ("R"), but this is a guess and should be confirmed.
- **Stop-loss distance**: The request said "get out if the trade goes against us too much" without a threshold. Assumed a fixed 5% below entry price. An ATR-based or swing-low-based stop is also plausible and may better fit "swing trading" but requires more design detail than was given.
- **Long-only vs. short**: Assumed long-only (buy on uptrend, exit on downtrend) since the request only mentions buying on up-trend and selling on down-trend, not short-selling.
- **Single position at a time**: Assumed no pyramiding/scaling — one open position max — since the request describes a single simple buy/sell cycle per trend turn.
- **Stop-loss vs. signal-exit ordering (revised per reviewer feedback)**: Rather than framing this as "stop-loss always overrides the next signal," each bar now evaluates exits in a fixed sequence: any signal-exit already queued from the prior close fills first at this bar's open, and only if the position is still open does the day's low get checked against the stop. This keeps intrabar stop protection meaningful (a big same-day drop still closes the trade at the stop price) without treating the stop as categorically senior to a pending signal exit. This is still a design choice, not a stated requirement, and should be confirmed with the stakeholder.
